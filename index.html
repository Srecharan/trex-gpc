Abstract

Automating leaf manipulation in agricultural settings faces significant challenges, including the variability of plant morphologies and deformable leaves. We propose a novel hybrid geometric-neural approach for autonomous leaf grasping that combines classical computer vision with neural networks through self-supervised learning.

Our method integrates YOLOv8 for instance segmentation and RAFT-Stereo for 3D depth estimation to build rich leaf representations, which feed into both a geometric feature scoring pipeline and a neural refinement module (GraspPointCNN). The key innovation is our confidence-weighted fusion mechanism that dynamically balances the contribution of each approach based on prediction certainty.

Our self-supervised framework uses the geometric pipeline as an expert teacher to automatically generate training data. Experiments demonstrate that our approach achieves an 88.0% success rate in controlled environments and 84.7% in real greenhouse conditions, significantly outperforming both purely geometric (75.3%) and neural (60.2%) methods.

System Architecture

Our hybrid approach combines three key components: vision pipeline, grasp point selection, and robot manipulation. The system processes stereo image pairs to output precise 3D grasp coordinates.

Multi-Stage Perception Pipeline

Instance Segmentation

Fine-tuned YOLOv8 on ~900 images, achieving 68% mAP@[0.5:0.95] for precise leaf mask generation.

Depth Estimation

RAFT-Stereo provides high-precision depth maps with sub-pixel accuracy for detailed 3D reconstruction.

MATLAB surface plot analysis showing leaf curvature distribution

Hybrid Grasp Point Selection

Traditional Computer Vision Pipeline

Geometric CV component uses Pareto optimization for leaf selection based on clutter score (35%), distance score (35%), and visibility score (30%). Grasp point selection employs flatness analysis, approach vector quality, and accessibility scoring.

Neural Refinement (GraspPointCNN)

Self-supervised learning where the CV pipeline acts as an expert teacher. The CNN processes 9-channel input features with spatial attention mechanism for optimal grasp point refinement.

Confidence-Weighted Integration

Dynamic integration strategy where ML influence varies (10-30%) based on prediction confidence, falling back to traditional CV (70-90%) for low-confidence predictions.
